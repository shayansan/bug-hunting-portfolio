# Methodology

This section explains the general approach I follow throughout my learning process in cybersecurity.

Before getting into specific technical topics like vulnerabilities, tools, or exploitation techniques, I believe it is important to build a clear and structured way of thinking. Cybersecurity is not only about knowing tools or memorizing techniques; it is mostly about understanding systems, analyzing behavior, and approaching problems with the right mindset.

Because of that, this section focuses on documenting the way I approach learning and technical analysis. It describes how I study complex topics, how I try to understand systems such as Linux and networking, and how I evaluate whether I actually understand a concept or not.

Another important goal of this section is to keep my learning process structured. Instead of jumping randomly between topics, I try to follow a consistent method: first understanding the foundations, then gradually moving toward more advanced areas like web security, vulnerability research, and bug hunting.

The files in this directory are not meant to be a full guide to cybersecurity. They simply document the way I personally approach learning and analyzing technical subjects while building my knowledge in this field.

Over time, this methodology will evolve as my understanding grows and as I move deeper into different areas of cybersecurity.
